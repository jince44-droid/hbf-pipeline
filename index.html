Setup Guide
Google Sheets + SharePoint · One-time setup
1

Google Sheet is already connected

The dashboard reads from your published Google Sheet automatically. Click ↻ Load Data in the top bar to fetch the latest data at any time. To update deals, edit your Google Sheet directly — then click Refresh to see changes in the dashboard.

2

Set your SharePoint base URL

Open SharePoint and navigate to your Company Profiles folder inside your Corp Dev document library. Copy the URL from your browser and paste it below. The dashboard will use it to generate a direct link to each company's folder in the Files tab of every deal drawer.

✓ Saved — links will appear in each company's Files tab
3

Create company folders in SharePoint

Inside your Company Profiles folder, create one subfolder per company using the exact company name (e.g. DELO Industrial Adhesives). The dashboard generates the link automatically — just drop files into the folder and they'll be accessible from the dashboard.

4

Enable the AI Agent

Paste your Anthropic API key in the top bar (starts with sk-ant-). The agent has full context on your live pipeline and can draft outreach emails, DD checklists, valuation memos, IC notes, competitive scans, and more.

5

Share with your team

Share this HTML file via email or put it on a shared drive. Each team member opens it in their browser — no installation needed. Everyone reads from the same Google Sheet. To edit deal data, update the Google Sheet directly. The dashboard auto-refreshes on load.

Strategic Fit Rating Criteria
H.B. Fuller Corporate Development · Official grading rubric
★★★★★Nearly Perfect Strategic Alignment
  • Fills a Top 20 Strategic Gap
  • Strong cross-sell opportunities with minimal customer overlap
  • Critical technologies or IP highly valuable
  • Strong cultural and operational compatibility — "Great Talent"
  • Immediate synergies possible
  • Strengthens core growth markets or access to strategic regions
★★★★Very Good Alignment or Significant Consolidation Opportunity
  • Product lines additive with little overlap
  • Expands market access or fills key portfolio gaps (Top 20)
  • Moderate technology synergies
  • Cultural fit manageable with integration planning
  • Significant operational or supply chain synergies possible
★★★Acceptable / Niche Fit
  • Focused on non-core but attractive adjacent market
  • Some technology or customer base relevance
  • Cultural integration more complex
  • Could be platform for growth with investment
  • Some synergies possible but not immediate
★★Limited Strategic Value
  • Unrelated products or markets
  • Heavy effort required to integrate or reposition
  • Minor synergy potential — more financial than strategic
  • Limited overlap with core capabilities
★No Strategic Alignment
  • Entirely different industry or technology
  • No synergy, no product overlap
  • Difficult cultural or operational integration
  • Would dilute focus or resource capacity
  • Primarily opportunistic with very high risk
